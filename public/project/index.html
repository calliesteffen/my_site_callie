Projects

Hawaiian Islands: Landcover and Watersheds

Introduction: This analysis will explore land use and watersheds for the big island of Hawaii. The land use and land cover data was primarily based on the interpretation of aerial photography from the 1970’s and 1980’s, with secondary sources including land use maps and surveys. Watersheds are shown for the 8 major islands and measured in square miles. See metadata (linked below) for specific watershed names and more detailed descriptions of what is included in the 7 categories of land cover.

PCA Analysis of Dairy Products

Introduction: PCA of USDA Food Group: Eggs and Dairy Products This Principle Component Analysis (PCA) explores relationships of nutrient variables of Dairy and Egg Products based off data from the United States Department of Agriculture. By performing a PCA, we can simplify the dimensions of the data while still explaining a majority of the variance Data source: U.S. Department of Agriculture, Agricultural Research Service. 2018. USDA Food and Nutrient Database for Dietary Studies 2015-2016.

Snowshoe Hares

Introduction: Snowshoe Hare Analysis of Age, Weight, and Sex This project contains an analysis of age, weight, and sex of captured Snowshoe hares (Lepus americanus) in Tanna Valley, Alaska. The study used the capture-recapture method from 1999-2002. Snowshoe hares experience population fluctuations of 8-11 years and are recognized as a keystone species in northern boreal forests. Data Source: Kielland K., F. S. Chapin, R. W. Ruess. 2017. Snowshoe hare physical data in Bonanza Creek Experimental Forest: 1999-Present.

Steelhead Salmon Passage at Bonneville Dam

Introduction: This project contains time series data for Steelhead Salmon passage across the Bonneville Dam in Oregon from 1939 - 2019. The analysis will include daily, seasonal, and yearly time series of salmon passage to visually and mathetmatically explore the fluctuations of the population over an 80 year time span. Data source: Columbia River DART, Columbia Basin Research, University of Washington. (2019). Adult Passage Graphics & Text. Available from http://www.
